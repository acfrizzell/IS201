Maryland

Fun Facts:

  • On April 28, 1788, Maryland became the 7th state to ratify the U.S. Constitution and join the Union
  • Annapolis, Maryland was the country's capital for less than a year in 1783
  • Named after Queen Henrietta Maria of England
  • Maryland produces the most blue crabs in the United States
  • Nickname is "The Old Line State"

Basic Statistics:

  • Population: 6.165 million
  • 19th largest state by population
  • 3rd most diverse state in the nation
  • 9th smallest state in terms of land mass
  • 5th most densely populated state with about 594 people per square mile

Economic Statistics:

  • 3rd lowest unemployment rate in the nation at 2.3%
  • 17th largest economy in the nation, accounting for 1.9% of total U.S. economy
  • GDP of $518.7 billion in goods and services (3rd quarter of 2023)
  • Highest median household income in the nation at $98,461

Basic Trivia

  • Capital: Annapolis
  • # of Counties: 23
  • Abbreviation: MD
  • Biggest City: Baltimore
